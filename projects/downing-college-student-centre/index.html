Butterfield Student Centre, Downing College

Butterfield Student Centre, Downing College

Cambridge, United Kingdom 2021–

Client: University of Cambridge, Downing College

The proposed new Student Centre at Downing College is located on the outer range of Downing’s campus, close to the College entrance on Regent Street. Responding to William Wilkins’ East and West ranges, the design adopts a contemporary version of his neo-Greek architecture, using proportions and a reduced tectonic expression rather than decorative embellishment. The building creates flexible spaces for group working, religious functions, events, social life and clubs, with generous, well lit interiors achieved through carefully proportioned window openings.

Read more

Within the College’s developing sustainability strategy, the project seeks to minimise embodied energy and energy use in operation. The proposed structure uses cross-laminated (CLT) and laminated veneer (LVL) panels for floors and structural walls, providing a robust yet low-carbon framework. The façades are to be built with large, self-supporting blocks of locally sourced stone. The second-floor pediment will be finished in hardwood cladding stained with a breathable, UV-protected wood stain, ensuring durability and stability. Drawing from traditional construction techniques while integrating modern high-performance membranes and coatings, the building aims for longevity, adaptability, and environmental responsibility. The scheme received planning permission in 2021 but has not yet been realised.
