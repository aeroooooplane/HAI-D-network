Office Building, Grosser Burstah

Office Building, Grosser Burstah

Hamburg, Germany 2014–2023

Client: Bilton Real Estate GmbH

This commission was won in an invited competition organised by the city. The new office building for the developer Bilton Real Estate GmbH is part of a wider project to rearrange the shape of the city streets to the north of St Nikolai Memorial, which were lost in the post-war development of the site. The wider master plan re-establishes a scale of city block that is more consistent with the older streets in this central quarter of the city, making it more permeable. The new building will provide 13,700 square metres of offices and retail space, over ten floors.

Read more

The site is in the centre of the old city. On one side, the building faces Grosser Burstah, an important city street with a nineteenth century scale. On the other side, the building faces a canal, closing an important view from the Trostbrücke and Nikolaibrücke bridges and standing in the context of adjacent historic buildings along the water edge including the medieval former town hall.

The aim of the design is to shape the mass of the building, so that its façades have the proportion and delicacy to address these different situations and contribute to the richness and quality of urban space that is so enjoyable in this part of Hamburg. The façades are clad in profiled terracotta tiles in a light buff colour close to the colour of adjacent historic buildings.

Caruso St John led the design to the planning stage, and prepared detailed drawings. The project was delivered by local architects Tchoban Voss Architekten.
